Overview

The Daet Line is a railway line operated by Daet Railway in the provinces of Camarines Norte and Camarines Sur, Philippines. The line extends 77 kilometers from Sipocot, Camarines Sur to Labo, Camarines Norte, serving a total of 24 stations. The railway provides an important transportation link between the municipalities of Labo, Basud, Daet, and Sipocot, connecting communities, schools, workplaces, and tourist destinations throughout the region.

Services

The Daet Line serves as the primary commuter railway for the Labo–Daet–Basud corridor. Most local trains operate at intervals of 44 minutes, with additional services during the morning peak periods. Five trains in each direction operate over the entire route between Labo and Sipocot every day. Additional services operate between Colasi and Sipocot to accommodate tourists in the Hamoraon area.

History

Planning and Construction

The Daet Railway Company was established in 1933 after receiving a franchise to construct a railway linking the town of Daet with the Manila Railroad Company's South Main Line.

Three possible routes were studied:

  • Northern Route — Via Santa Elena, connecting to Lopez Station in Quezon Province.
  • Central Route — Following the inland corridor between Mount Labo and Mount Colasi before reaching Sipocot.
  • Seaside Route — Following the coast of San Miguel Bay and connecting to Sipocot.

The seaside route was ultimately selected due to its lower construction costs and ability to serve a greater number of coastal communities. However, the alignment required the construction of tunnels along the mountains around Hamoraon and Cotmo area.

Opening of the Railway

Construction began in 1935.

The first section between Daet and Sipocot opened to passenger service on June 3, 1939, providing the first rail connection between Camarines Norte and the rest of the national railway network.

After the war, work resumed on the northern extension. The final section from Daet to Labo was completed and opened on April 1, 1948, establishing the present-day route of the Daet Line.

Modernization

Beginning in the 1950s, steam locomotives were gradually supplemented by diesel railcars from Japan, improving reliability and reducing operating costs.

The introduction of the KiHa 40, KiHa 11, and later the KiHa 25 fleet modernized passenger services and allowed for increased service frequencies in the Daet urban area.

Today, the Daet Line remains the backbone of public transportation in Camarines Norte and serves thousands of commuters each day.

Former Connecting Lines

Mercedes Line

The Mercedes Line was a tramway branch connecting Daet with the coastal municipality of Mercedes.

Originally opened in 1905 as the Daet Tramway, the 6-kilometer line linked the commercial center of Daet with the port facilities of Mercedes.

When the Daet Line was extended to Labo in 1948, the tramway was extended by 1.6 kilometers to connect with the newly opened Lag-on Station.

The line was acquired by Daet Railway in 1950 and renamed the Mercedes Line. Passenger and freight services operated for two decades before severe damage caused by Typhoon Sening led to its closure in 1970.
